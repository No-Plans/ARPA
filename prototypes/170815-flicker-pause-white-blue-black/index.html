ARPA Studio is a creative direction agency founded by Jasmine Raznahan in 2011, after graduating from the Royal College of Art. A multi-disciplinary practice, the studio works across print, digital and three dimension and has created campaigns, lookbooks, brand identities, packaging, publications, websites and social media strategies for clients include Céline, Chloé, Victoria Beckham, Missoni, Stella McCartney, John Lobb, Fabrizio Viti, Hayward Gallery & Tate Publishing.

ARPA Studio’s work is innovative in approach and explorative in form, regardless of medium. Led by a conceptually-driven approach, the studio’s capacity to offer panoramic solutions has seen the development of longstanding relationships with brands, working with them to strengthen and enhance their visual language across all platforms.

Jasmine is also the founder and creative director of Noon  a bi-annual magazine that explores the space between art, commerce and digital culture. It has been described as a publication that ‘quickly established itself as a beautifully produced magazine that subverts tradition and places innovative collaboration at its forefront’ (Jefferson Hack). Featuring collaborations with Juergen Teller, Zoe Ghertner, Charlie Engman, Mel Bles, Mark Borthwick and Vanessa Reid, these creative partnerships have gone on to enrich and inform the studio’s commercial work, cultivating a rich network of the industry’s leading talent from which to draw upon.
